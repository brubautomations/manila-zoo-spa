[ANIMALS & ZONES (WHAT TO SEE)]
The Manila Zoo features over 500 animals and 10,000 plants in modernized, glass-fronted enclosures.

Zone 1 (Elephants): Features the Asian Elephant sanctuary. (Note: If guests ask about "Mali", acknowledge her legacy fondly. Mali was the beloved resident elephant for decades who passed away in late 2023).

Zone 2 (Hippo Pools): Features Hippopotamus in aquatic viewing areas.

Zone 3 (The Aviary): A massive walk-through dome. Home to the critically endangered Philippine Eagle (Pithecophaga jefferyi), Macaws, Hornbills, and Ostriches.

Zone 5 (Savanna): Open plains featuring Zebras, Reticulated Giraffes, and the critically endangered Tamaraw (Mindoro Dwarf Buffalo).

Zone 6 (Apex Predators): Glass-enclosed habitats for African Lions, Bengal Tigers, and "Kois" (the famous White Siberian Tiger donated in 2021).

Zone 8 & 11 (Reptiles): Features the Indo-Pacific Saltwater Crocodile, Philippine Crocodile, and various pythons and turtles.

Zone 14 (Primates): Features Long-tailed Macaques and monkeys in interactive jungle gyms.

Other Attractions: The Butterfly Garden, Botanical Gardens, Animal Museum, Zoo Lagoon, Center Island Interactive Fountains, and the Children's Park.

[CONSERVATION EFFORTS]

Rehabilitation: The zoo reopened in late 2022 after a multi-billion peso renovation. It now features a state-of-the-art Sewage Treatment Plant that prevents pollution into Manila Bay.

Mission: We act as an urban botanical sanctuary, a massive carbon sink for the city, and a rescue/breeding center for endemic species like the Philippine Eagle and Tamaraw.
